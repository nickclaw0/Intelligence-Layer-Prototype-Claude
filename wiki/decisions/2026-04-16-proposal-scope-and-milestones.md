The scope-and-budget review reshaped the proposal into a base recommendation with priced add-ons.

## Decision

[The base scope covers strategy foundation, message architecture, creative platform, and launch planning, with two add-ons: a validation sprint and launch-toolkit expansion]^[src:fde0e7fa]. Payment is [tied to outputs: 30% at kickoff, 30% after positioning approval, 25% after creative platform, 15% at final toolkit delivery]^[src:fde0e7fa], over [twelve weeks for the base, fourteen with the validation sprint]^[src:fde0e7fa].

## Rationale

Strategy foundation and message architecture are essential because [if they are weak, every later asset becomes more expensive as teams keep re-litigating the story]^[src:fde0e7fa]. The eight-week question was answered directly: [you can make slides in eight weeks, but you cannot responsibly align medical, legal, commercial, and creative in eight without rework]^[src:fde0e7fa].

## Rejected alternatives

- [Making the launch toolkit itself optional was rejected; instead the optional part is the number of assets, not the toolkit]^[src:fde0e7fa].
- [Keeping a broad market read was traded down to focused stimulus checks, losing statistical confidence but keeping directional confidence]^[src:fde0e7fa].

## Consequences

A claims-and-voice guardrail system was added as essential governance so [medical, legal, and commercial can make decisions faster]^[src:fde0e7fa].
