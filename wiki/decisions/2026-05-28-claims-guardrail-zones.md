The MLR risk session set the rules that let strategy and creative move without pretending review risk is not real.

## Decision

Language is sorted into [three zones: approved claim language, strategic language for internal use, and prohibited language]^[src:5dbcb391], with [a claims guardrail appendix of phrase, use case, risk, and safer alternative]^[src:5dbcb391], and [patient support materials reviewed under a different lens than branded promotional materials]^[src:5dbcb391].

## Rationale

Separating brand-strategy language from promotional claims means [the internal platform can use "sleep confidence" while external execution carries claim-specific substantiation]^[src:5dbcb391], and [creativity is preserved through structure and metaphor visually while copy stays precise]^[src:5dbcb391].

## Rejected alternatives

- [Unsupported phrasings are prohibited: cure, guaranteed sleep, "natural sleep" and "non-habit forming" unless supported, anything implying no next-day effects, plus "reset your brain" and "switch off insomnia"]^[src:5dbcb391].
- ["Sleep that feels possible again" is allowed only in context, not next to efficacy claims in a way that implies certainty]^[src:5dbcb391].

## Consequences

["The Steady Night" survives as a campaign platform if it is not used as a clinical promise]^[src:5dbcb391], and the guardrails fold into the next proposal version.
