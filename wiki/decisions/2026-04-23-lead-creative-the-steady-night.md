The creative territories review chose the lead expression of the positioning.

## Decision

["The Steady Night" is the lead creative territory. "Tomorrow Reclaimed" becomes a future-facing message module. "Quiet Return" informs tone but is not the platform]^[src:6a1a3142].

## Rationale

The Steady Night is [ownable without overpromising and the safest medically]^[src:6a1a3142], and its [structured gradients, measured spacing, and rhythm line can become a brand device]^[src:6a1a3142]. The guiding principle was that [the brand should perform confidence, not sleepiness]^[src:6a1a3142].

## Rejected alternatives

- ["Quiet Return" was beautiful but read as premium wellness and risked looking like a meditation app]^[src:6a1a3142].
- ["Tomorrow Reclaimed" may imply a stronger outcome than can be supported, so it was held back as a later patient-campaign module]^[src:6a1a3142].

## Consequences

The team will [develop the lead system across HCP, patient, and internal launch materials, including a deliberately bad version to show leadership why restraint matters]^[src:6a1a3142].
