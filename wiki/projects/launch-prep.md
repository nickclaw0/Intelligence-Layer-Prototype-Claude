The first agency engagement for Velorixa: build a brand platform and launch proposal for an insomnia treatment ahead of launch.

## Scope

The business pressure behind the brief is clarity. [Velorixa has a promising insomnia treatment but no shared story across medical, commercial, and leadership, and wants a launch that feels bigger than a product detail aid]^[src:623851f2]. The engagement was structured around [four phases: foundation, creative system, launch planning, and activation toolkit, each with decision points]^[src:623851f2]. Research is deliberately lean: [a validation sprint with patient and HCP stimulus testing rather than a large segmentation study]^[src:623851f2].

Scope was split at the budget review into a base recommendation and two add-ons. [The base covers strategy foundation, message architecture, creative platform, and launch planning; the optional layer is deeper research and expanded asset development]^[src:fde0e7fa]. [Strategy foundation, message architecture, and a claims-and-voice guardrail system were defended as essential because weak foundations make every later asset more expensive]^[src:fde0e7fa].

## Phases and outputs

- Commercials: [payment tied to outputs at 30% kickoff, 30% after positioning approval, 25% after creative platform, 15% at final toolkit delivery]^[src:fde0e7fa].
- Timeline: [twelve weeks for the base scope, fourteen if the validation sprint is included]^[src:fde0e7fa].
- First four weeks once underway: [week one immersion and stakeholder interviews, week two platform refinement, week three message architecture, week four claims and voice guardrails]^[src:8e725cdb].
- Decision owners named at go: [Jonah for brand, Priya for medical, Carlos for HCP, Rachel for insights, Maya for final escalation, Darren for procurement]^[src:8e725cdb].
