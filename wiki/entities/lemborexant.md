Lemborexant is a marketed dual orexin receptor antagonist for insomnia. It appears in the Velorixa evidence library as a clinical comparator, not as a Velorixa product. Nothing in the raw sources states whether Velorixa's own molecule is lemborexant, so this page does not assert that link.

## Overview

Two research sources in the corpus study lemborexant directly. [In participants with comorbid insomnia and mild obstructive sleep apnea, lemborexant reduced insomnia severity without significantly impacting next-morning cognitive functioning and alertness, whereas zolpidem showed negative effects on attention and memory]^[src:c9f89994]. A second source examines [lemborexant's effect on sleep quality and its association with morning function]^[src:7f123b6a].

## Key facts

- Relevant to the brand's "clear morning" pillar: [lemborexant's next-morning cognition profile in mild untreated OSA did not show the impairment seen with zolpidem]^[src:c9f89994].
- The comparison in that trial was [lemborexant 5mg and 10mg against zolpidem and placebo in 410 participants]^[src:c9f89994].
