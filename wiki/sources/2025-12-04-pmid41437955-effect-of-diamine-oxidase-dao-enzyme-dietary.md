Research source on a dietary-supplement approach to insomnia tied to a specific genetic variant. A niche, mechanism-of-action study; category-adjacent rather than central to the prescription brand.

## Summary

An exploratory randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial of a diamine oxidase supplement in people whose insomnia is associated with variants of the AOC1 gene, which encodes the DAO enzyme. [101 patients (73.4% women, mean age 48.3) were randomised to 28 days of the DAO product (12.6 mg/day) or placebo, with insomnia tracked by the Insomnia Severity Index and the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index]^[src:de5d5e68]. [Decreases in ISI scores were similar in both groups, but severe insomnia at day 28 was higher in the placebo group (5.9% vs 2%), and PSQI improvement at day 28 was greater in the DAO group]^[src:de5d5e68]. [In the DAO group on a once-daily regimen, sleep efficiency and use of sleep medication improved significantly versus baseline, and in melatonin users the DAO group showed greater ISI improvement that persisted to day 28, leading the authors to conclude DAO supplementation improved insomnia symptoms in the presence of AOC1 variants and showed synergy with melatonin]^[src:de5d5e68]. This is an early, narrowly-scoped finding tied to a genetic subgroup, so it carries little weight for Velorixa beyond illustrating how segmented the self-care end of the category is.

## Key extracts

- [PSQI improvement at day 28 was greater in the DAO group, and severe insomnia was less common than on placebo]^[src:de5d5e68].
- [The authors report a synergy between DAO supplementation and melatonin, in an exploratory study limited to AOC1 variant carriers]^[src:de5d5e68].
