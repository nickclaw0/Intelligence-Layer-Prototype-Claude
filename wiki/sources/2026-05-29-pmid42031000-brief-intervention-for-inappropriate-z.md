Research source on deprescribing sleep medication in primary care. Relevant to the brand's responsible-use and safety framing.

## Summary

A brief GP-delivered intervention aimed to reduce inappropriate z-hypnotic use in older adults. [Comparing an immediate-treatment group with a delayed control, the intervention reduced inappropriate z-hypnotic use from 68.9% at baseline to 27.78% after treatment, improved anxiety and depression scores, kept sleep quality stable, and did not affect cognitive function]^[src:5ed073f3]. The finding supports a stewardship narrative: reducing inappropriate hypnotic use without worsening sleep.

## Key extracts

- [Inappropriate z-hypnotic use fell from 68.9% to 27.78% after the intervention]^[src:5ed073f3].
- [Sleep quality remained stable and cognition was unaffected while mood scores improved]^[src:5ed073f3].
