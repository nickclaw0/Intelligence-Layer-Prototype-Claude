Research source quantifying a downstream health consequence of insomnia. Useful for the burden-of-disease framing without being a brand claim in itself.

## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis pulling together observational and Mendelian randomization evidence on whether insomnia raises stroke risk. [Searches across PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Google Scholar, and Web of Science up to March 2024 fed a random-effects meta-analysis of studies that evaluated insomnia diagnosis or symptoms against stroke as an outcome]^[src:9bd3c254]. [Insomnia symptoms showed no significant association with overall stroke (HR 1.16; 95% CI 0.88-1.52), but were associated with a modest increase in ischemic stroke risk (HR 1.07; 95% CI 1.04-1.10) rated high certainty, while a clinical diagnosis of insomnia was associated with a substantially higher risk of any stroke (HR 1.45; 95% CI 1.32-1.61)]^[src:9bd3c254]. The split between symptoms and a formal diagnosis matters: it suggests diagnosed, persistent insomnia carries the clearer cardiovascular signal. For the brand this reinforces that insomnia has real downstream cost, which supports the seriousness of the category without ever being repurposed as a Velorixa efficacy claim.
