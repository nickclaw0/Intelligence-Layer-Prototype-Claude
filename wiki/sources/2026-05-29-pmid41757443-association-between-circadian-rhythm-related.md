## Summary

A bidirectional Mendelian randomization study on circadian rhythm-related behaviour and breast cancer. [Using GWAS data from the MRC Integrated Epidemiology Unit, it found longer sleep duration associated with higher breast cancer risk (OR 1.33, P < 0.001), while being a morning person (OR 0.76, P = 0.003) and getting up in the morning (OR 0.71, P < 0.001) were protective]^[src:135f70e1]. The only insomnia-facing result runs in the reverse direction: [breast cancer patients were more likely to experience insomnia (OR 1.01, P < 0.001)]^[src:135f70e1]. The framing is oncology and chronotype, not insomnia treatment, so this source has little bearing on Velorixa beyond sitting loosely in the circadian cluster of the evidence base.
