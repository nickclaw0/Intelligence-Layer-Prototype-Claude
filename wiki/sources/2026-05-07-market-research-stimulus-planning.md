Source page for the research stimulus planning meeting, which set what the validation sprint is allowed to decide. Fake transcript for the prototype.

## Summary

The discipline was to keep the study small and decision-focused: [test comprehension, credibility, emotional relevance, and actionability, capturing preference but not letting preference alone decide, because the most liked language is not always the most useful or compliant]^[src:99785cfc]. Four stimulus boards were proposed: [core positioning, patient message house, HCP message house, and creative look-feel]^[src:99785cfc], with the name tested separately from the messages.

## Key extracts

- [Sample: twelve chronic-insomnia patients, eight primary care physicians, six sleep specialists, including patients who have and have not tried treatment]^[src:99785cfc].
- [The positioning would change if "sleep confidence" reads as vague, trivial, or unsupported, or if patients hear it as guaranteed success]^[src:99785cfc].
- [Success signals are behavioral: patients say "that sounds like what I need to talk to my doctor about" and HCPs say "I know which patients this is for"]^[src:99785cfc].
- [Timing: recruit next week, field the following week, readout five business days later, delivered as implications and go/no-go recommendations, not a transcript dump]^[src:99785cfc].
