## Summary

The kickoff set ambition before deliverables. The client's core pressure is [clarity, because the company has a promising insomnia treatment but no shared story across medical, commercial, and leadership]^[src:623851f2]. The agency's early read is that [the richer territory is helping people feel in control of their nights again, since the market is already crowded with functional sleep promises]^[src:623851f2], while the client is firm that [Velorixa is a treatment brand that needs scientific credibility and must not drift into lifestyle wellness]^[src:623851f2]. The creative direction reconciles those as [calm, clinical, and human at the same time, framed as emotional precision rather than softness]^[src:623851f2].

The name is settled but carries a tension. [Velorixa is legally locked, and its "velo" prefix reads as movement or velocity, which the agency proposes to frame as a return to rhythm rather than speed]^[src:623851f2]. Claims discipline was set early: [the brand cannot promise sedation or immediate resolution unless claims support it]^[src:623851f2].

## Key extracts

- [The proposal will be structured around four phases: foundation, creative system, launch planning, and activation toolkit, each with decision points]^[src:623851f2].
- [Research will be a lean validation sprint with patient and HCP stimulus testing, not a large segmentation study, to catch language risk and find the promise with the most pull]^[src:623851f2].
- [Leadership wants a proposal it can approve quickly, with clear phases, clear outputs, and a transparent budget]^[src:623851f2].
- [The guiding principle stated by the client: enough evidence to decide, not enough research to avoid deciding]^[src:623851f2].
- [The phrase "sleep like a baby" is banned from creative]^[src:623851f2].
