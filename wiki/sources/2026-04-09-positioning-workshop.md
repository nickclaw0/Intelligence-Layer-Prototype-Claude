Source page for the positioning workshop, the meeting that chose Velorixa's strategic frame. Fake transcript for the prototype.

## Summary

Three routes were tabled and one won. [Route two, "Restoring sleep confidence," was judged the most distinctive and ownable; route three "A clearer path through insomnia" was strong for HCPs but less memorable for patients; route one "The night can change" was too broad]^[src:7e2a71d4]. The recommendation was framed as ownership, not a menu: [Velorixa should own restored sleep confidence, expressed through calm onset, next-day relevance, and medically grounded support]^[src:7e2a71d4].

## Key extracts

- [Confidence is defined through three components: confidence to discuss insomnia, confidence to start treatment, and confidence to evaluate progress]^[src:7e2a71d4].
- [Confidence helps medical review because it connects to behaviors and expectations, not a claimed clinical endpoint]^[src:7e2a71d4].
- [The visual language avoids moon-and-pillow cliches, favoring rhythm, quiet structure, and a transition from uncertainty to steadiness]^[src:7e2a71d4].
- [A "do not do" page goes into the brand guidelines so internal teams do not default to stars and blankets]^[src:7e2a71d4].
- [Decision: route two is the lead, route three becomes support language, route one is out]^[src:7e2a71d4].
