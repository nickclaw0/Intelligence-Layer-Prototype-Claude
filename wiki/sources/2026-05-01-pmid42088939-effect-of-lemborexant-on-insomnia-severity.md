## Summary

This is a post hoc analysis of a phase 3 trial (E2006-G000-304, NCT02783729) looking at lemborexant in people who have insomnia alongside mild obstructive sleep apnea, a combination the authors call COMISA. [The analysis included 410 participants with insomnia and mild OSA, randomised to lemborexant 5 mg, lemborexant 10 mg, zolpidem tartrate extended-release 6.25 mg, or placebo over one month]^[src:c9f89994]. [Mean change from baseline in Insomnia Severity Index total score improved with lemborexant 5 mg (-7.1), lemborexant 10 mg (-7.7), and zolpidem (-8.7) versus placebo (-5.7), with lemborexant 10 mg and zolpidem reaching statistical significance (lemborexant 10 mg vs placebo P = 0.0278; zolpidem vs placebo P = 0.0011)]^[src:c9f89994]. The cognition signal is the part most useful to the brand: [lemborexant did not significantly affect next-morning cognitive functioning and alertness, while zolpidem negatively affected power of attention and quality of memory at days 2/3 and speed of memory retrieval at days 30/31 versus placebo]^[src:c9f89994]. The authors conclude [lemborexant reduced insomnia severity without significantly impacting next-morning cognition and alertness in patients with mild untreated OSA]^[src:c9f89994].

## Key extracts

- [Lemborexant 10 mg significantly improved insomnia severity versus placebo (ISI change -7.7, P = 0.0278)]^[src:c9f89994].
- [Lemborexant did not significantly impact next-morning cognitive functioning or alertness, whereas zolpidem impaired several cognitive domains versus placebo]^[src:c9f89994].
