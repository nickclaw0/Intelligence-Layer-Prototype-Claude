Research source on herbal and integrative options for chronic insomnia, compared head-to-head against a benzodiazepine. Adjacent to the prescription brand and useful for category context, with comparator claims that are MLR-relevant if ever reused.

## Summary

A randomised controlled parallel-group trial compared two integrative options against a conventional hypnotic in chronic insomnia. [Sixty adults with chronic insomnia were randomised over four weeks to Suk-Sai-Yat (a traditional Thai herbal remedy), Cannabis sativa oil (Deja formula), or lorazepam]^[src:e89a8bb3]. [All three groups showed significant improvement in Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index scores with no significant difference between groups: Suk-Sai-Yat fell from 12.3 to 6.6, Cannabis sativa oil from 13.6 to 3.68, and lorazepam from 14.4 to 5.8, all p < 0.001]^[src:e89a8bb3]. [Quality-of-life scores improved significantly in the integrative therapy groups, only mild adverse events were reported, and the authors concluded both herbal interventions had comparable efficacy to lorazepam with favourable safety profiles]^[src:e89a8bb3]. For Velorixa this is context rather than support: it speaks to the appetite for alternatives to standard hypnotics, which is the space a well-tolerated prescription option is trying to occupy.
