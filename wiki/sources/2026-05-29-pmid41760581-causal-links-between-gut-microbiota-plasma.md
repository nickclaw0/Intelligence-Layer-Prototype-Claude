Research source: a genetics-based causal analysis of the gut-brain axis in insomnia. Mechanistic and insomnia-relevant, though far from the brand's positioning work.

## Summary

A Mendelian randomization study testing whether gut microbiota influence insomnia, and whether plasma metabolites sit on that causal path. [Using GWAS data from 5,959 individuals for gut microbiota and 8,299 for plasma metabolites, with insomnia outcomes from Pan-UKB, GERA, and FinnGen covering 9,007 cases and 871,802 controls, the analysis identified 10 gut microbiomes and 35 plasma metabolites potentially associated with insomnia]^[src:da46906f]. [Increased abundance of certain microbiomes, such as the species CAG-145 sp000435615, was linked to higher insomnia risk, and mediation analysis found that 3-ethylcatechol sulphate levels significantly mediated the effect, explaining up to 31.49% of the total effect]^[src:da46906f]. [The authors conclude this highlights the role of gut microbiota in influencing insomnia risk through specific plasma metabolites and may inform therapeutic targets for managing sleep disorders]^[src:da46906f]. For Velorixa this is background biology rather than a positioning input, but it sits in the same gut-brain-axis cluster as a couple of the other research sources.
