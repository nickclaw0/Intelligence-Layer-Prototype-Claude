Source page for the MLR risk and claims guardrails session, which set the rules that govern what the brand can say. Fake transcript for the prototype. This content is MLR-relevant; treat it as gated for human review before any external use.

## Summary

Legal opened by asking to [separate brand strategy language from promotional claims]^[src:5dbcb391]. The agreed structure is [three zones: approved claim language, strategic language for internal use, and prohibited language]^[src:5dbcb391]. The internal platform can keep "sleep confidence" while [external execution carries claim-specific substantiation]^[src:5dbcb391], and [creativity is preserved through structure and metaphor visually while copy stays precise]^[src:5dbcb391].

## Key extracts

- [Prohibited language includes cure, guaranteed sleep, "natural sleep" unless supported, "non-habit forming" unless supported, anything implying no next-day effects, "reset your brain," and "switch off insomnia"]^[src:5dbcb391].
- ["Sleep that feels possible again" may be acceptable in context, but not next to product efficacy claims in a way that implies certainty]^[src:5dbcb391].
- [The deliverable is a claims guardrail appendix: phrase, use case, risk, safer alternative]^[src:5dbcb391].
- [Patient support materials need a different review lens than branded promotional materials]^[src:5dbcb391].
- ["The Steady Night" can stay as a campaign platform if it is not used as a clinical promise]^[src:5dbcb391].
