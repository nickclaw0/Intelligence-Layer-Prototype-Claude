Research source on lemborexant and the patient experience of sleep quality. Directly relevant to the brand and to the "restored sleep confidence" positioning, and MLR-sensitive because it carries efficacy claims.

## Summary

A post hoc analysis of two global phase 3 trials asked whether lemborexant changes how patients rate their own sleep quality, and what that rating tracks with. [The analysis pooled Study 303, a 12-month randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial, and Study 304, a 1-month randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, active-comparator trial, in patients with insomnia]^[src:7f123b6a]. [In Study 303 at month 6, subjective sleep quality improved more with lemborexant 5 mg and 10 mg than placebo, and in Study 304 at month 1 both doses again improved sleep quality versus placebo]^[src:7f123b6a]. The finding most useful to positioning is the association: [changes in morning alertness showed the strongest association with changes in subjective sleep quality]^[src:7f123b6a]. That gives an evidence-grounded thread between how rested someone feels in the morning and how they rate the night, which maps onto the brand's daytime-functioning story.

## Key extracts

- [Lemborexant improved patient-reported sleep quality versus placebo across both a 1-month and a 12-month phase 3 trial]^[src:7f123b6a].
- [Morning alertness was the parameter most strongly associated with improved subjective sleep quality]^[src:7f123b6a].
