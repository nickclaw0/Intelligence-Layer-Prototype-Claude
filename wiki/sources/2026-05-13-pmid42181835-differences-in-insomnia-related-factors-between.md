Research source: an observational comparison of insomnia symptoms across age groups. Directly relevant to the insomnia category.

## Summary

The study compares insomnia-related symptoms between working-age (30-64) and older (65+) adults. [Across 1,655 survey participants classified as normal sleepers, subsyndromal insomnia, or insomnia syndrome, working-age adults showed significantly worse scores for all nocturnal and daytime symptoms, and fatigue was a better discriminator than daytime sleepiness across age groups]^[src:be770f60]. The authors conclude [age-specific assessment and treatment strategies are needed]^[src:be770f60].

## Key extracts

- [Working-age adults reported worse nocturnal and daytime symptoms than older adults]^[src:be770f60].
- [Fatigue, more than daytime sleepiness, distinguished insomnia types]^[src:be770f60].
