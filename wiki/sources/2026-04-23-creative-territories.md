Source page for the creative territories review, which chose the lead expression of the positioning. Fake transcript for the prototype.

## Summary

Three territories all laddered to the same idea; emphasis decided the winner. ["The Steady Night" was chosen because it is ownable without overpromising and the safest medically, where "Tomorrow Reclaimed" may imply a stronger outcome than can be supported and "Quiet Return" risked looking like premium wellness]^[src:6a1a3142]. The governing principle: [the brand should perform confidence, not sleepiness]^[src:6a1a3142].

## Key extracts

- [The Steady Night uses structured gradients, measured spacing, and a rhythm line that can become a brand device, paired with human language and warmer secondary colors]^[src:6a1a3142].
- [Avoid dashboards, moons, stars, and glowing bedroom windows; the world is quiet but not sleepy]^[src:6a1a3142].
- ["Tomorrow Reclaimed" becomes a future-facing message module; "Quiet Return" informs tone only]^[src:6a1a3142].
- [Develop the lead system across HCP, patient, and internal launch materials, including a bad version to show leadership why restraint matters]^[src:6a1a3142].
