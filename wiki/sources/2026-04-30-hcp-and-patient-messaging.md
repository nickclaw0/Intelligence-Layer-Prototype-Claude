Source page for the HCP and patient messaging alignment, which resolved how one promise speaks to two audiences. Fake transcript for the prototype.

## Summary

The challenge raised was that [the patient message was emotionally strong while the HCP message still read like a rational proof list]^[src:6c47f361]. The fix is [a two-layer system: patient promise "sleep that feels possible again" and HCP role "identify, discuss, and support appropriate patients," bridged by next-day impact, which matters to both audiences]^[src:6c47f361]. HCP framing avoids blame: [insomnia burden is often under-expressed in the visit, not "doctors are failing to ask"]^[src:6c47f361].

## Key extracts

- [Next-day functioning is clinically relevant and emotionally meaningful, so it is the bridge between audiences]^[src:6c47f361].
- [Patient support needs a responsible expectation-setting module with clear benefit-risk framing, no minimizing and no burying]^[src:6c47f361].
- [Field teams get a message card set: patient fit, treatment expectations, safety conversation, and follow-up]^[src:6c47f361].
- [Same core promise, different proof language, so patient and HCP decks feel related without using the same headline everywhere]^[src:6c47f361].
