The branded experience model that turns a medication into a support system spanning education, initiation, support, and adherence.

## Definition

The thesis is to [make treatment feel navigable before it feels promotional, reducing cognitive load at every step for tired, skeptical, overloaded patients]^[src:5023bbd7]. The operating model has [four-plus layers: orient, prepare, start, sustain, reflect]^[src:5023bbd7], with [a branded patient onboarding sequence across the first 45 days from a day-0 start card to a day-45 review prompt]^[src:5023bbd7]. The build runs [from core tools in the first 60 days to a CRM spine, then HCP enablement, then optimization]^[src:5023bbd7].

## Why it matters here

The design rule is [less friction, more follow-through: one decision per screen, teach only what matters now, normalize questions and starts]^[src:5023bbd7]. In launch planning the team flagged that [the patient experience work should not wait too long, or the brand launches with awareness but no support path and loses the confidence idea]^[src:b61683b9], and proposed [a first-night expectation tool built as expectation-setting, not advice beyond label]^[src:b61683b9].
