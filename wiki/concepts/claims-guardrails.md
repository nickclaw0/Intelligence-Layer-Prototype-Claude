The governance system that lets medical, legal, and commercial move faster by separating strategic language from promotional claims.

## Definition

The guardrails sort language into [three zones: approved claim language, strategic language for internal use, and prohibited language]^[src:5dbcb391]. Prohibited language includes [cure, guaranteed sleep, "natural sleep" unless supported, "non-habit forming" unless supported, anything implying no next-day effects, and phrases like "reset your brain" or "switch off insomnia"]^[src:5dbcb391]. The discipline was set at kickoff, where [the brand cannot promise sedation or immediate resolution unless claims support it]^[src:623851f2], and [the phrase "sleep like a baby" is banned]^[src:623851f2].

## Why it matters here

The internal platform can use "sleep confidence" while [external execution needs claim-specific substantiation]^[src:5dbcb391]. A headline like "sleep that feels possible again" [may be acceptable depending on context, but not if it sits next to product efficacy claims in a way that implies certainty]^[src:5dbcb391], which is why the system adds [context rules and a claims appendix of phrase, use case, risk, and safer alternative]^[src:5dbcb391]. Patient support materials [need a different review lens than branded promotional materials]^[src:5dbcb391], and patient messaging carries [a responsible expectation-setting module with clear benefit-risk framing, no minimizing and no burying]^[src:6c47f361].
