The launch model: sequence credibility before reach so the brand removes hesitation before it scales the promise.

## Definition

The thesis is that [the fastest launch is not the loudest, it is the one that removes hesitation first, lowering efficacy hope, safety concern, and patient shame together]^[src:d7ff25ad]. The approach is to [start narrow on high-intent moments, prove simply, then scale carefully to avoid hype that triggers concern]^[src:d7ff25ad]. Audiences are prioritized as [sleep specialists for credibility, PCPs for volume with simplification, and chronic strugglers for urgency and reassurance]^[src:d7ff25ad], and channels weighted toward [HCP field and patient search over mass media]^[src:d7ff25ad]. Sequencing runs [from a proof base at T-6 to T-4, market prep at T-3 to T-1, launch in months 0-3, then scale]^[src:d7ff25ad].

## Why it matters here

In the planning sprint the team set the dependency order as [positioning approval, message house approval, claims guardrails, then channel planning]^[src:b61683b9], and recommended [a calm build: condition legitimacy first, brand promise second, treatment conversation third, not a giant splash that says we solved insomnia]^[src:b61683b9]. Payers are handled separately so they [do not drive the public brand voice]^[src:b61683b9].
