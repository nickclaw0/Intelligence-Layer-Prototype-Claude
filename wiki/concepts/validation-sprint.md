The lean qualitative research step that pressure-tests the positioning before it becomes a launch system.

## Definition

The intent set at kickoff was [a lean validation sprint with patient and HCP stimulus testing, not a giant segmentation study, to catch language risk and find the promise with the most pull]^[src:623851f2]. The design tests [comprehension, credibility, emotional relevance, and actionability, capturing preference but not letting preference alone decide]^[src:99785cfc], across [four stimulus boards: core positioning, patient message house, HCP message house, and creative look-feel]^[src:99785cfc]. The sample is [twelve chronic-insomnia patients, eight primary care physicians, and six sleep specialists, including patients who have and have not tried treatment]^[src:99785cfc].

## Why it matters here

Reducing the sample was the budget compromise: [the team keeps the learning objective but trades statistical confidence for directional confidence around language, comprehension, and emotional resonance]^[src:fde0e7fa]. Success signals are behavioral, where [patients say "that sounds like what I need to talk to my doctor about" and HCPs say "I know which patients this is for"]^[src:99785cfc]. The positioning would change [if "sleep confidence" reads as vague, trivial, or unsupported, or if patients hear it as guaranteed success]^[src:99785cfc].
