The message architecture that keeps Velorixa coherent across audiences without sounding identical.

## Definition

The principle is [one brand promise, four proof languages: stakeholders do not need different brands, they need different proof]^[src:9bb80978]. The shared promise is held central while [the evidence, tone, and action requested change by audience: patients get emotional permission and clarity, HCPs get clinical confidence and fit, payers get burden and appropriate use]^[src:9bb80978]. Each audience gets a message house, for example the [patient house "you are not failing, the night can feel safer, tomorrow matters" and the HCP house "identify, match, support"]^[src:9bb80978].

## Why it matters here

It resolves the tension Carlos Mendes raised, that [the patient message was emotionally strong while the HCP message still read like a rational proof list]^[src:6c47f361]. The agreed model is [a two-layer system: patient promise "sleep that feels possible again" and HCP role "identify, discuss, and support appropriate patients," bridged by next-day impact]^[src:6c47f361]. Discipline is enforced by [messaging QA rules: reduce shame, define appropriate fit, avoid overpromising, link night to day, and close with one action]^[src:9bb80978].
